Participation is All You Need
Toward distributed cognitive substrates beyond the transformer paradigm
1. The limits of attention
The transformer architecture demonstrated that a single mechanism — scaled attention over token sequences — could unify language understanding and generation. Eight years later, transformers dominate AI. Yet a structural tension has emerged: increasingly centralized, stateless between sessions, dependent on knowledge frozen at training time.
In most current product implementations, a transformer has no memory beyond its context window. Every session begins from zero. The intelligence lives in data centers. Users are treated as endpoints. These are not inevitable failures — they are consequences of design choices that the participation paradigm proposes to complement.
2. What participation offers
Participation generates persistent state.
Participation distributes knowledge across time.
Participation decentralizes intelligence.
When a user participates in a network, structured behavioral signals accumulate into a living representation that persists between sessions — not a context window but a profile that evolves.

4. Attention ≠ Participation
Most social networks function less as participation systems and more as attention extraction systems. Their primary metric tends to be time on screen, clicks, engagement — not contribution, coordination, or emergence.
Social networks have largely failed to produce collective intelligence — not because it is impossible, but because their architecture was optimized for a different goal.
The productive opposition is not individual ↔ crowd. It is attention ↔ participation.
Intelligence as representation — the system builds a model and responds. Intelligence localized in weights. Benchmarkable.
Intelligence as dynamics — the system evolves toward better configurations. Intelligence in the trajectory. The question is not "what does it know?" but "where does it go?"
5. Collective reasoning is reorganization
participants → interactions → network reorganization → emergence
The result is not an answer. It is a transformation of the state of the system. A city reasons by reorganizing. A market converges toward a state. An ant colony does not compute the optimal path — the path appears.
We have spent eight years optimizing the individual cognitive mechanism — attention — and almost no work has been done on the social substrate in which cognition is embedded.
6. The four primitives
Persistent identity — recognizable across time by accumulated participation history, not by name.
Structured broadcast — participants emit structured signals about their current state to the network.
Composition — the intersection of two participants' states produces something neither held alone.
Incentive alignment — participation generates value for participants directly, rather than accruing centrally.
7. The human is a component
In the current dominant architecture:
Human → AI → Response
The human is exterior to the cognitive system. Even "human-in-the-loop" frames the human as a correction mechanism, not a constitutive component. What the participation paradigm proposes:
Human ↔ Network ↔ Modules ↔ AI
The human is not merely a user of the system.
The human becomes a component of the system's dynamics.
The fundamental unit tends toward participation itself rather than the individual. Identity is not the input — identity emerges as output. A city is a movement. A language is a movement. YourMine aims to be a movement: the pattern persists as participants enter and leave.
The AI field tends to ask: how do we produce a better representation of the world?

The participation paradigm asks: how do we create the conditions for a world to self-organize?
8. Expansion, not amplification
Participation creates more participation if and only if it expands the configuration space of the system.
Without this condition, participation produces amplification — not emergence. And amplification of an existing pattern is not intelligence. It is repetition at scale.
Diversity — more variations within the same rules. Same interactions, same structures. Still amplification.
Expansion — new dimensions of rules become possible. Interactions that were structurally impossible before. The coordinate system changes.
n spheres → n options (amplification) n spheres → 2ⁿ configurations (expansion)
The composition primitive — peerSection() — converts the system from amplifier to expander. Without it, spheres are apps. With it, spheres are dimensions.
9. The open question
Emergence is not a property of participation.
It is a property of the rewriting of the rules of participation themselves.
The system is running. The observation is ongoing. Whether this constitutes genuine emergence remains an empirical question. But the test is clear: not whether participation increases. Whether new things become possible.

Working Paper — Yong, JdC — 2025
What Is a Payoff?
Participatory Value and Categorically-Valued Open Games
Abstract
Classical game theory rests on a hidden ontological commitment: every value relevant to strategic behavior is representable as a real number. We argue this commitment fails for a class of systems — participatory, distributed, compositional — where the reward for engagement is not a quantity but a structure: a relational object produced by composition, destroyed by defection, and irreducible to any scalar without loss of information. We propose categorically-valued open games — open games where payoff functions map strategy profiles to objects in a symmetric monoidal category C — as the minimal formal extension required to represent structural value. The classical case is recovered from the thin symmetric monoidal category induced by the total order on the reals. The framework admits incomparable equilibria. The central claim: incomparability is not a bug. It is what value looks like when it is structure rather than quantity.
1. The Hidden Assumption
Every payoff function in classical game theory has the form Ui : Σ → ℝ. This is so standard it appears not as an assumption but as a definition. The assumption carries two substantive commitments. Representability: every value can be assigned a real number. Completeness: any two values can be compared — for any outcomes a and b, either a ≥ b or b ≥ a. These are not mathematical necessities. They are assumptions about what value is. And they fail for systems where what engagement produces is not a quantity but a structure.
2. Where Scalar Utility Fails
Relational value. When two people discover a common context — a game, a discipline, a way of working — something is created: a relational object, a shared structure that enables certain interactions and recognitions. This object has structure. It can be extended. It is partially ordered. It can be destroyed by withdrawal. There is no canonical way to assign a real number to it that preserves its compositional properties. Any numerical assignment collapses distinctions that matter. This is not a measurement limitation — it is a structural mismatch.
Sen and completeness. Amartya Sen's critique of welfare economics targets precisely the completeness assumption (1979, 1985, 1999). Forcing complete preference orderings on incommensurable values distorts the value structure. The agent who cannot rank more political freedom against less health is not irrational — they are recognizing that the comparison the theory demands does not exist. Sen's framework admits genuinely incomparable outcomes. The partial order on C is the formal structure Sen's critique requires.
Formal antecedents. Aumann (1962) showed utility functions need not exist when preferences are incomplete. Bewley (2002) developed Knightian decision theory for agents who cannot rank all alternatives. Dubra, Maccheroni, Ok (2004) characterized expected utility for incomplete preferences. What this literature lacks is a game-theoretic extension. Our framework provides this in the language of open game theory.
3. Background: Open Games
Definition 3.1 (Ghani et al. 2018). An open game G consists of: players N with strategy sets Σi; a play function P : Σ → X; a coplay function C : Σ × R → ×i ℝ mapping strategy profiles and an external context r ∈ R to real-valued payoffs; an equilibrium predicate E : R → 𝒫(Σ). Open games compose sequentially (G;H) and in parallel (G⊗H), forming a symmetric monoidal category Game. Nash is the degenerate case where R is a singleton.
Limitation: the coplay function maps to ℝ and uses total numerical comparison. It cannot represent games where payoffs are objects with compositional structure and partial order.
4. Categorically-Valued Open Games
Definition 4.1 (Payoff Category). A payoff category is a symmetric monoidal category (C, ⊗, I) with: a partial order ≤C on objects compatible with ⊗; an initial object ∅C; directed completeness.
Example 4.2 — Classical case
Recovered from the thin symmetric monoidal category induced by the total order on ℝ ∪ {−∞}. Morphism a → b exists iff a ≤ b. Monoidal product is max. Initial object is −∞. Because the order is total, all objects are comparable — classical game theory is a special instance of categorical structure.
Example 4.4 — YourMine payoff category CYM
Objects: finite diagrams of sphere contributions — formal colimits of peerSection() outputs indexed by subsets of active spheres. Morphisms: natural transformations. Monoidal product: diagram union. Unit I: mandatory sphere social.sphere.js. Initial object ∅: empty diagram. Partial order: diagram inclusion D1 ≤ D2 iff D1 is a subdiagram of D2. Not total — two diagrams with different sphere types and no inclusion relation are incomparable.
Definition 4.5 — Equilibrium predicate (revised for partial orders)
A categorically-valued open game G over C has equilibrium predicate:

σ ∈ E(r) iff ∀i, ∄ σ′i : Ci(σ′i, σ-i, r) >C Ci(σ, r)

where >C is the strict part: a >C b if a ≥C b and b ≱C a.

When the order is total this reduces exactly to Nash. When payoffs are incomparable, neither strictly dominates — the equilibrium is stable. Incomparability is native to the definition.

Why not Pareto-maximality? Pareto shifts the game from non-cooperative to cooperative: it assumes players coordinate. Absence of strict deviation is the minimal generalization of Nash to a partial order, preserving individual rationality without assuming coordination.

Does an equilibrium always exist? Not automatically. Directed completeness of C is the load-bearing condition: it guarantees best responses exist even in a partial order. Without it, equilibrium existence is not guaranteed.

Note: not enrichment in the sense of Kelly (1982). We replace payoff values, not hom-sets. We use "categorically-valued" to avoid terminological confusion.
Proposition 4.7. When C is the thin category on ℝ, Definition 4.5 recovers Ghani et al. exactly.
5. The Defection Object
Proposition 5.1. In a categorically-valued open game where payoffs are colimits of contribution diagrams, a player who contributes nothing receives ∅C — the initial object. Proof: colimit of empty diagram is the initial object.
In classical theory defection produces a lower number — the player still exists in the payoff space. In categorically-valued theory defection produces ∅C — not a reduced quantity but an absence of structure. You do not become slightly less valuable. You withdraw from the structure entirely. In CYM this is the empty identity surface. In the classical thin category, ∅ = −∞, recovering the classical representation.
6. Composition and Information Loss
Proposition 6.1. Categorically-valued open games over C form a symmetric monoidal category Game(C). When φ : C → ℝthin exists, there is F : Game(C) → Game(ℝ).
Proposition 6.2 — Information loss
For CYM, any monoidal functor φ : CYM → ℝthin forgets structural distinctions — it identifies structurally distinct diagrams assigned the same scalar. Any such φ must assign numerical values to sphere types; different choices produce different orderings; no choice is canonical since sphere types are qualitatively distinct. Therefore any φ is not fully faithful. Many functors exist — cardinality, weight-based. All collapse structural distinctions the categorical representation preserves.
7. Participatory Equilibrium
Definition 7.1. A participatory equilibrium is a maximal equilibrium section σ* : R → Σ — no other section σ′ satisfies σ′(r) >C σ*(r) for some r while σ′(r) ≥C σ*(r) for all r.
Existence Program 7.2
If Σ is compact and coplay is order-continuous, directed completeness of C guarantees best responses exist. A Zorn's lemma argument on partial sections ordered by componentwise domination yields a maximal section. Full proof requires specifying the topology on the space of sections. Stated as program, not theorem.

Equilibria are more numerous here than in Nash: incomparability itself creates stability — a player with no strictly better deviation has no individual reason to move. However this abundance is conditional on directed completeness of C.
Proposition 7.3 — Embedding of Nash
When C is the thin category on ℝ, Nash equilibria embed into participatory equilibria and the two coincide. Proof: total order → >C is standard strict inequality → absence of strict dominance is exactly the Nash condition.
Proposition 7.5 — Incomparable equilibria
In Game(CYM), there exist participatory equilibria σ*1 and σ*2 incomparable in ≤C. Proof: take profiles producing D1, D2 with different sphere types and no inclusion relation — incomparable by diagram inclusion, both may be maximal sections.
The equilibrium predicate makes incomparability structurally native. A player does not deviate when no strictly better option exists. In a partial order, incomparable options are not strictly better — they are different. The equilibrium is stable not because the payoff is maximal in an absolute sense but because no available deviation is unambiguously superior.
8. Incomparability Is Not a Bug
Incomparability is what value looks like when it is structure rather than quantity.
Classical theory treats incomparable outcomes as theoretical embarrassment — a sign that the model is incomplete, that a common scale has not yet been found. The entire project of welfare economics is in part a project of forcing comparison. We propose the opposite. Incomparability is the faithful representation of participation patterns that produce qualitatively different relational structures. A framework that forces comparison loses the structure. Nash is the special case where all values happen to be comparable. Participatory equilibrium is the general case.
9. YourMine as Motivating Instance
Id(A,B) = colims ∈ σA ∩ σB peerSections(A,B)
An object in CYM. Empty when A and B share no spheres. Grows under diagram inclusion. Partially ordered. Two users with different shared sphere compositions produce incomparable identity surfaces — incomparable participatory equilibria. The mandatory sphere social.sphere.js implements the monoidal unit I — always present, preventing complete withdrawal.
No central authority — context produced by players, not assigned from outside.
No fixed identity — identity is the colimit of all shared sphere compositions.
No universal ranking — participation patterns are incomparable in the partial order.
No defection payoff — withdrawal produces ∅, not a lower number.
10. Viscoelastic Value: An Interpretation
The framework in sections 4–7 is mathematically self-contained. The viscoelastic theory of value (The Eye and the Hand, ch. 4) provides a physical grounding that is not required for validity. Economic activity is a process of articulating distinct cognitive universes. Value emerges as the force of a metabolic process with specific physical structure: viscoelastic behavior — combining elastic energy storage and viscous dissipation.
Elasticity → Velocity → Liquidity → Utility → Value
Elasticity — structural memory, stored potential against entropy. Velocity — temporal activation of stored potential through the network. Liquidity — free circulation where elastic tension relaxes. Utility — directional conversion of liquid energy into organized action. Value — the integral: effort accumulated over time, metabolic tension maintained against entropy.
The scalar payoff of classical theory is the terminal state of this sequence. Two outcomes with the same scalar payoff may be in radically different metabolic states: one with high elasticity and low velocity, another with low elasticity and high liquidity. Same number. Not the same outcome. Categorical payoffs represent states in the generative sequence. Incomparable equilibria represent agents in different metabolic phases — not better or worse, but different in kind.
11. Open Problems
Existence. Complete the existence program 7.2 — specify the topology on sections, verify the extension condition for Zorn.
Uniqueness. Conditions on C guaranteeing unique participatory equilibria.
Efficiency. Social welfare as colimit over players' payoff objects. Price of anarchy in this setting.
Forgetful functor. Which structural information is lost by F : Game(C) → Game(ℝ)?
Formal viscoelastic payoff category CV. Objects as viscoelastic state vectors with precise metabolic coupling rules for ⊗.
Formal verification. Can participatory equilibria be verified in Lean or Agda?
12. Conclusion
The question is not how to generalize Nash equilibrium.
The question is what a payoff is.

Classical theory: a number.
Our answer: a composable object.
Classical theory embeds representability and completeness — appropriate for markets, inappropriate for participatory systems where value is relational, compositional, structurally irreducible to a scalar. Sen saw the completeness failure philosophically. Our framework extends it to strategic interaction. Incomparability is not a theoretical failure. It is what value looks like when it is structure rather than quantity.
